How I Used AI to Rebuild My Shoulder (and Rewire My Recovery Mindset)

David O'Regan
• April 19, 2025 • 7 min read

A personal story about leveraging AI as a training partner, recovery coach, and progress tracker

In July 2023, I underwent a SLAP labrum repair and biceps tenodesis after years of chronic shoulder pain as a former weightlifter. Post-surgery, I expected a slow and steady road back to movement. What I didn't expect was that the most consistent, insightful, and adaptive coach I'd have during recovery would be an AI.

This post is about how I found ChatGPT, how I used it as a personal recovery assistant, and how it transformed my training mindset as a Senior Engineering Manager working on GitHub Copilot.

How I Found It

Like most people recovering from surgery, I spent countless hours searching for rehab protocols, Reddit success stories, and YouTube physio tips. But nothing quite met me where I was: someone with deep training experience, post-op restrictions, and a mix of ambition and fear.

I turned to ChatGPT. Initially, it was a curiosity—"can this help me plan a shoulder-safe workout?" I asked. The response was precise, cautious, and better than anything I'd cobbled together online. I kept going.

How I Trained It

What started as basic Q&A turned into something far more advanced: an intelligent, personalized rehab agent.

ChatGPT remembers long-term context through its memory and agent framework. Over time, I trained it by:

  • Logging my post-op timeline and exact surgery details (SLAP repair + biceps tenodesis)
  • Describing my flare-up history, especially after overuse or failed reintroduction attempts
  • Providing detailed reports of each workout—reps, sharp pain triggers, fatigue points
  • Clarifying movement goals like unassisted L-sits, red band overhead press, and eventual Muay Thai return
  • Sharing my energy levels, flare-up responses, and mental state week-to-week

Behind the scenes, this allowed ChatGPT to:

  • Develop a persistent memory of my injury history, movement tolerances, and goals
  • Auto-tune intensity, volume, and rest intervals based on real feedback loops
  • Adapt every session into a state-aware plan
  • Reflect back progress trends, training plateaus, and smart milestones

"It acted like a structured agent with task memory, variable awareness (e.g. pain = 3/10 or skipping = pain-free), and constraints baked into every decision. It remembered that I swapped yellow bands for red. That I couldn't perform Y-raises. That I needed all warm-ups to be skipping-based."

This wasn't a chatbot. It was a fully stateful system with progressive planning logic.

How I Use It Now

Today, ChatGPT is my active recovery log, movement strategist, and psychological stabilizer. When I feel stuck, it reflects back my progress. When I feel ready to push, it reins me in just enough to keep me consistent.

Our current system is a one-day-on, one-day-off structure with skipping as a warm-up, rehab finishes, and progressive calisthenics strength work. It knows my milestones (like July 9, my 1-year post-op target). It helps track when I'm ready for deeper work, like reintroducing shadowboxing.

And importantly: it remembers my hesitations. It doesn't push volume when it knows I'm worried about overload. It helped me recognize when three sessions a week were enough—and when I might be ready to try more.

Why This Matters (Especially at GitHub)

Working on GitHub Copilot, I see daily how AI helps developers move faster. But using it in this deeply personal, physical, human context showed me something else:

AI doesn't have to replace a coach. It can be a thinking partner. It can hold your history, your parameters, your fears, and your ambitions, and help you move through complexity with structure and clarity.

This project wasn't just about healing my shoulder. It helped me reclaim a daily rhythm of self-discipline, patience, and body awareness.

"And it never missed a session."

Technical Details

  • Tools used: ChatGPT-4 with memory and agent-like context tracking
  • Training style: Calisthenics + mobility + progressive load management
  • Voice: Conversational, human, intuitive
  • Final result: Still in progress, and still getting better

Conclusion

The intersection of AI and physical recovery has been a profound experience for me. As someone who works on AI tools professionally, experiencing the technology as a user in such a deeply personal context has given me new insights into how these systems can augment human capabilities rather than replace them.

My journey is still ongoing, but the partnership with AI has already made a significant difference in my recovery. It's given me structure, accountability, and perhaps most importantly, a data-driven way to see my progress when subjective feelings might suggest otherwise.

Have you used AI in unexpected ways for personal growth or recovery? I'd love to hear your experiences in the comments below.
